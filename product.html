The Enchanted Forest

By Lena Hartwood

$89.74

Step into a world where magic breathes through the trees and secrets whisper in the wind.

When sixteen-year-old Elara stumbles upon a hidden path in the woods behind her grandmother’s cottage, she enters the Enchanted Forest—a place of breathtaking beauty and ancient mystery. But the forest is more than it seems. Shadows move between the trees, time bends and folds, and creatures of legend walk under the moonlight.

As Elara uncovers her connection to this mystical realm, she learns that the balance between light and darkness is crumbling. An ancient curse is awakening, and only she holds the key to restoring harmony—if she can survive the trials that await.

Blending lush world-building with heart-pounding adventure, The Enchanted Forest is a spellbinding tale of courage, destiny, and the power of believing in what lies beyond the ordinary.

Perfect for fans of The Night Circus and The Starless Sea, Lena Hartwood’s mesmerizing debut will transport readers into a world they’ll never want to leave.

Reviews

Review

Lena Hartwood’s The Enchanted Forest completely transported me. The world-building is lush and immersive, and I could almost smell the pine needles and hear the whispers of the trees. Elara is such a relatable heroine—brave, uncertain, and full of heart. This book had everything: mystery, danger, magic, and a touch of wonder that reminded me of being a kid again. Absolutely enchanting!

Review

This book is like stepping into a dream. The writing is poetic, and the descriptions of the forest are breathtaking. I loved the themes of destiny and courage, but the pacing dipped a bit in the middle chapters. Still, the ending tied everything together beautifully, and I closed the book feeling uplifted and inspired. I’d recommend it to fans of Erin Morgenstern or Holly Black.

Review

The premise is fantastic—a girl discovering a hidden, magical world tied to her family’s past—but I wished for a bit more action. The story leans heavily on atmosphere and emotion, which is great if you love slower, lyrical fantasy. The ending was satisfying though, and I’d definitely read a sequel if there is one.

Review

The Enchanted Forest is one of those rare books that lingers in your mind long after you finish. The forest itself feels like a character—mysterious, alive, and full of secrets. I loved how Hartwood balanced dark, eerie moments with warmth and hope. It’s the perfect read for a rainy afternoon and a must for anyone who believes in the magic of nature. This content is a little bit longer.
